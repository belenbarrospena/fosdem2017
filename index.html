Mapping design activities to roles

  • User researchers explore user needs, problems and context
  • Content editors and information architects produce content
  • Information architects and interaction designers decide on structure and behaviour
  • Interaction designers determine navigation and interface components
  • Visual designers design the interface looks (layout & graphic design)

No we don't

FOSS projects are mainly worrying about their looks and their logos

Why are FOSS projects not investing any effort in the non-visual aspects of software design?

Why should we invest on the non-visual aspects of software design?

Removing the non-visual design roles effectively removes user-centered design

User-centered design helps us spot problems in our software before we build it

Forcing workarounds upon users to compensate for our omissions and misunderstandings does not work

User-centered design engages users as equal partners in the process of making software

Without this true partnership with users, free and open source software does not stand a chance
